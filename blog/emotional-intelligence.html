Researching EQ and IQ

1. What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence refers to a person's ability to identify, evaluate, control, perceive, and express emotions. As well as the ability to relate to others, having a high EQ boosts empathy, self-awareness, the quality of your inter-personal relationships and the ability to maintain those relationships through a mutual understanding.

2. How is EQ different to IQ?

If I were to use a metaphor, I would compare EQ and IQ to delivering a speech. IQ is the substance of the speech; it is the carefully chosen topic, the curated research, and the visual representation of Slides or PowerPoints. However, EQ is the deliverance of the speech; it is the charismatic speaking voice, the neatly-kept personal appearance, and, most importantly, the witty and inspiring writing.

IQ measures your intelligence, knowledge, analytic thinking, reasoning, and logic. EQ measures your ability to empathise with others, your control over your emotions, and your ability to understand others. Essentially, as cheesy as it may sound, if IQ is represented by the brain, then EQ must be represented by the heart.

3. Why is EQ important?

There was a great example of EQ being the equivalent to 'street-smarts'. Emotional intelligence is a necessary skill, particularly in a human society where being social is a huge obstacle for some. It is important to be able to look at a person and identify whether they are feeling sad or upset, to empathise with them, and to build or maintain solid relationships. While I'm sure it does exist, it is hard to find a job that doesn't deal with any other people at all - working in a team with low EQ is like working in a library when you can hardly read. While it isn't impossible, it certainly makes things difficult for you.
